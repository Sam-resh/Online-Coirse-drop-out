User Research Report
Discovery & Insights
6-week mixed-methods research sprint across 40 interviews, 1,200 surveys, 15 diary studies, and 2.1M behavioral sessions.
Research Methods
🎙️ In-Depth Interviews — n=40
20 dropouts, 10 completers, 10 churned-paid subscribers. 45–60 min Zoom sessions. Conducted in English, Hindi, Tamil.
📊 Quantitative Survey — n=1,200
In-app prompt 30 days post-enrollment for learners under 50% complete. Validated patterns from qualitative phase.
📓 Diary Studies — n=15, 14 days
Daily voice memo: "Did I learn today? Why/why not?" Rich context for the Day 1–7 behavioral patterns.
📡 Behavioral Analysis — 2.1M sessions
Cohort retention curves, drop-off heatmaps within lessons, enrollment source analysis, device-type breakdowns.
Key Findings
Finding 1: The Day 3 Cliff is Universal
The Day 1→Day 3 retention drop (82% → 34%) is the steepest cliff across ALL segments — course type, device, geography, and price tier. It signals a system-level habit failure, not a content problem.
Finding 2: Learners Have No "Place" for Learning in Their Day
71% of dropouts have no fixed time for learning. 78% of completers have a fixed ritual (with coffee, during commute, after dinner). The product must help learners build a time slot — not just remind them to return.
Finding 3: Missing a Day Feels Like Total Failure
Survey: 44% of learners who stopped said "I lost my momentum / messed up my streak." The product must make recovery emotionally safe. Missing one day must not feel like course failure.
Finding 4: Social Accountability is Latent Demand
74% of users said they'd prefer starting a course with a cohort when shown a mock. 51% said they'd feel guilty dropping out if peers watched their progress. We've never offered this.
Finding 5: Completers Behave Differently From Day 1
Completers: (1) Start within 24h of enrollment, (2) Watch 3+ lessons in first session, (3) Have a re-entry pattern after gaps. Product must accelerate "completer behavior" in new users.

📄 v1.4 Engineering Review
🔥 Feature 1: Learning Streak System
P0 — RICE: 22.95

A daily learning streak counter that increments each day a user completes ≥1 lesson. Breaks if user misses a day (1 grace day per 7-day streak).

Acceptance Criteria
✓ Streak counter visible on home screen and course player header
✓ Streak increments when ≥1 lesson completed in a calendar day (user's local timezone)
✓ "Streak shield" — 1 free grace day per 7-day streak
✓ Streak breaks trigger specific "restart" flow, not generic notification
✓ Milestones at 7/30/100 days trigger full-screen celebration
✓ Offline completions sync on next connection; vacation mode pauses (not breaks) streak
⚡ Feature 2: Smart Resume Engine
P0 — RICE: 31.5

When a user returns after ≥48h away, they land on a personalized "pick up here" screen with context and a one-tap continue button.

Acceptance Criteria
✓ Triggers when user re-enters after ≥48 hours away
✓ Shows: lesson thumbnail, title, timestamp ("You stopped at 4:32"), time to finish
✓ Motivational context: "You're 68% through Module 3 — almost there!"
✓ Primary CTA: "Resume Now" | Secondary: "Go to Course Home"
✓ A/B test: full-screen modal vs. top-of-page banner
📝 Feature 3: Commitment Contract
P0 — RICE: 24.0

At course enrollment, user selects a weekly time commitment and optionally adds an accountability buddy email. System generates a personalized pacing plan.

Acceptance Criteria
✓ Commitment step inserted in enrollment flow (after payment, before Course Home)
✓ Three options: 30 min/wk, 1 hr/wk, 2 hr/wk
✓ Optional accountability buddy email — double opt-in required
✓ System shows estimated completion date based on commitment level
✓ "Commitment check" notification if user falls 2+ weeks behind plan
🎉 Feature 4: Milestone Celebrations
P0 — RICE: 54.0

Full-screen animated celebrations at 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% course completion with shareable badges for LinkedIn and WhatsApp.

Acceptance Criteria
✓ Triggers automatically when user crosses 25/50/75/100% threshold
✓ 100% celebration includes confetti, certificate preview, and share options
✓ Shareable PNG card sized for LinkedIn, Twitter, and WhatsApp
✓ Analytics event fires on each celebration shown and on each share action
✓ Can be disabled in accessibility settings
👥 Feature 5: Cohort-Based Enrollment
P1 — RICE: 7.56

Users can join a cohort — a group of 10–25 learners starting the same course on the same date, with shared feed and peer accountability.

Acceptance Criteria
✓ "Start with a cohort" option on enrollment page alongside "Start at my own pace"
✓ Cohort sizes: 10–25 learners. Auto-fill; new cohort opens when full
✓ Shared feed: celebrate milestones, post questions, encourage peers
✓ Weekly cohort summary email with peer progress highlights
✓ Cohort feed opt-in only; display name separate from account name

Risk Assessment
Risks & Mitigations
Identified risks with probability, impact ratings, and specific mitigation strategies for each.
Streaks cause anxiety or shame in professional learners
Tone testing for all UX copy before launch. Vacation mode bypass available. Full opt-out offered in settings. "Restart" flow is warm and encouraging, not punishing.
Medium Prob High Impact
Commitment contract step slows enrollment funnel
A/B tested with control before full rollout. Guardrail: enrollment completion rate monitored daily. Rollback in <2 hours if enrollment drops >5% in 48h window.
Medium Prob High Impact
Accountability buddy emails feel spammy to recipients
Double opt-in required before buddy's email is stored. Clear sender address (hi@learnos.com). Easy one-click unsubscribe. SPF/DKIM properly configured.
Low Prob Med Impact
Streak data lost due to Redis service outage
Redis cluster with multi-AZ replication. Manual recovery procedure in runbook. SLA: recover streak data within 4 hours of incident. Communication template ready for users affected.
Low Prob High Impact
Cohort communities become negative or toxic
Community guidelines displayed at cohort join. Report + mute tools available from Day 1. Moderation queue staffed. Trust & Safety review required before cohort goes to >10% traffic.
Low Prob High Impact
AI Pacing Engine model performs poorly on edge-case learner types
Human-in-the-loop fallback for all model recommendations. Model confidence threshold: only serve recommendation if P > 70%. Monitor model performance weekly with DS team. Users can override pacing plan at any time.
